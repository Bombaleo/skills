SIA members only · finding reproduced below

Contingent workers are roughly a fifth of the workforce

Staffing Industry Analysts — Workforce Solutions Buyer Survey, Americas · most recent results

The finding

Buyers surveyed by SIA report that contingent workers make up a median of about 20% — and an average of roughly 22% — of their total workforce.

How the calculator uses it: when you don't know your contingent labor spend, the "Estimate it" tab applies a contingent-share benchmark to your headcount to produce a starting figure. This is an illustrative planning value — replace it with the client's actual spend whenever it's known.

ASA members only · finding reproduced below

What a staffing bill rate is made of

American Staffing Association — “What goes into your bill rate?”

The finding

A staffing agency's bill rate is the worker's pay rate plus a markup that covers employer payroll taxes, workers' compensation and other insurance, statutory benefits, recruiting and overhead, and the agency's margin. Converting a long-tenured temporary worker to a permanent hire removes that recurring markup.

How the calculator uses it: it underpins the temp-to-perm conversion driver — the savings from eliminating the recurring agency markup on workers who have effectively become permanent.

Staffing Industry Analysts — “Benchmarks: Candidate fraud top of mind for contingent workforce programs”

SIA reports that 54% of companies experienced some type of identity-related fraud in their contingent program. Supports verified worker identity and approved time capture.

Percentage-of-spend is the dominant VMS pricing model

Staffing Industry Analysts — “How to evolve your VMS strategy”

SIA reports that fee as a percentage of spend remains the dominant VMS pricing model (about 65% of programs), and that VMS adoption among 1,000-plus-employee firms has risen to roughly 79%. Supports the 1.5%-of-spend platform-cost assumption.
