What rate cards are, in our model. The per-supplier, per-position pay-and-bill structure that turns a worker's clocked time into a posted bill amount. Each row carries a pay rate (what the supplier pays the worker), a markup (district × position × tier, with a defined precedence), and the engine output: bill rate. Rate cards live on a SupplierContract and are the input to ContingentEngagement.billRate; the engagement carries the immutable snapshot at intake.

What we have today. The rate-card primitive ships inside the supplier contract wizard (Step 3) and on the supplier details page as the RateCardsCard accordion. Twenty positions per supplier, status pill, district-markup tab strip rolled up to an effective district rate, a position-override field, an optional Solid/Outstanding/Epic tier ladder, and the bulk multi-select editor. Underneath it sits pages/pricing-config.jsx: a separate "Pricing Configuration" surface where pay premiums, holiday pay, employer contributions, tenure deductions, markup, and tax bands are authored as named groups that bind to a contract. The API layer (/pricing-rules CRUD, rate_change inbox kind) is fully described; the surfaces that close the loop are not.

What this list is. Every remaining job to take rate cards from "single-currency hourly with one markup column" to a structure that can model a four-country, multi-classification, effective-dated rate schedule against an MSA. Tasks are grouped by the four roles that touch a rate card: Admin (configures it), Manager (requests and approves changes), Agency (prices the work), Worker (sees the pay side, where policy allows). Foundations that cross roles sit in 00 · Foundations.

Architecture · embed, don't add

Every remaining task in this list lands in one of four existing files: pages/supplier-contract.jsx (wizard step 3), pages/supplier-contract-sections.jsx (RateCardsCard + ScRateCardEditorPanel), pages/pricing-config.jsx (named pricing groups), or pages/inbox.jsx (rate_change approvals). Settings → Configuration (pages/settings-config.jsx) already hosts the CSV upload, the tax engine, and the policy bus — nothing new there. Zero new top-level files are required to close this scope.

Section 04

Worker · not chasing

3 not chasing

Workers see what they're paid through Dayforce HCM, not through Flex Work — their pay statement, accruals, and tenure milestones already live in the parent suite. The handful of rate-card capabilities below sit further outside our scope; we name them so reviewers know they were considered and consciously declined.

Not chasing

Worker-facing rate transparency on assignment detail

FG and VNDLY both expose the worker's pay rate inside their worker portals. Our worker mobile is positioned to point at Dayforce HCM for pay statement; surfacing rate again inside Flex Work duplicates the source of truth and invites discrepancies between HCM payroll and VMS billing.

Fieldglass historically supports reverse-auction rate flows where suppliers bid down on a posting. Vendly does not chase this; it has limited adoption inside their book; reduces to a race-to-the-bottom in commodity categories. We're not building it.
